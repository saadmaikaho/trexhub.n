If your business isn't showing up on Google when potential customers search for what you offer, you're losing sales every single day. SEO (Search Engine Optimisation) is how you fix that — and it's one of the highest-ROI marketing investments you can make.

## Why SEO Matters More Than Ever in Nigeria

Internet penetration in Nigeria is growing rapidly. More Nigerians are searching Google before making purchasing decisions — whether it's finding a real estate agent in Lagos, a logistics company in Abuja, or a fashion brand in Port Harcourt. If you're not on page one, you're essentially invisible.

## The 3 Pillars of SEO That Actually Move the Needle

### 1. Technical SEO — The Foundation

Before anything else, your website needs to be technically sound:

- **Page speed**: Nigerian internet speeds vary. Your site must load in under 3 seconds on mobile.
- **Mobile optimisation**: Over 80% of Nigerian internet users browse on mobile. A poor mobile experience kills your rankings.
- **Crawlability**: Google needs to be able to find and index your pages. Broken links, missing sitemaps, and poor site structure all hurt you.

### 2. Keyword Research — Target What Nigerians Actually Search

Generic keywords like "digital marketing" are too competitive. Focus on:

- **Local keywords**: "digital marketing agency Lagos", "SEO services Abuja"
- **Long-tail keywords**: "how to run Facebook ads for real estate in Nigeria"
- **Commercial intent keywords**: "best [service] in [city]"

Use tools like Google Keyword Planner, Ubersuggest, or Ahrefs to find keywords with decent search volume and manageable competition.

### 3. Content — Answer the Questions Your Customers Are Asking

Google rewards websites that genuinely help users. Create content that:

- Answers specific questions your target customers have
- Covers topics your competitors haven't addressed well
- Is updated regularly with fresh, accurate information

## How Long Does SEO Take?

Honest answer: 3–6 months to see meaningful results, 6–12 months to see significant traffic growth. SEO is a long-term investment, but the compounding returns are worth it — unlike paid ads, organic traffic doesn't stop when you stop paying.
