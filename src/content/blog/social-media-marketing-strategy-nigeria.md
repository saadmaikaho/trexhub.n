Most Nigerian businesses treat social media like a notice board — they post when they remember, share whatever feels relevant, and wonder why they're not getting customers from it. Here's how to do it properly.

## Step 1: Choose the Right Platforms

You don't need to be everywhere. Focus on where your customers actually spend time:

- **Instagram**: Best for fashion, food, beauty, lifestyle, real estate, and any visually-driven business
- **Facebook**: Best for reaching 30–55 year olds, local businesses, and community-driven brands
- **LinkedIn**: Best for B2B, professional services, corporate brands, and recruitment
- **TikTok**: Best for reaching under-35s, entertainment, and brands willing to invest in video content
- **Twitter/X**: Best for tech, media, finance, and brands that want to join trending conversations

Pick 2 platforms maximum when starting out. Do them well before expanding.

## Step 2: Define Your Content Pillars

Your content should serve 3 purposes:

1. **Educate** — teach your audience something valuable related to your industry
2. **Engage** — spark conversation, ask questions, share relatable content
3. **Convert** — showcase your services, share testimonials, make offers

A good content mix is roughly 60% educate/engage, 40% convert. If you're always selling, people tune out.

## Step 3: Post Consistently (Not Just Frequently)

Consistency beats frequency. Posting 3 times a week every week is better than posting 7 times one week and disappearing for a month. Create a content calendar and stick to it.

## Step 4: Engage With Your Audience

Social media is a two-way conversation. Reply to every comment. Respond to DMs quickly. Engage with your followers' content. The algorithm rewards accounts that generate genuine interaction.

## Step 5: Track What's Working

Look at your analytics monthly:

- Which posts got the most reach and engagement?
- Which posts drove the most profile visits or DMs?
- What time of day does your audience engage most?

Double down on what works. Cut what doesn't.

## The Shortcut: Combine Organic + Paid

Organic social builds trust and community over time. Paid social (Facebook/Instagram Ads) accelerates growth and drives direct conversions. The best strategy uses both together.
