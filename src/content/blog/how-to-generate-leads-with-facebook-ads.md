Facebook has over 33 million active users in Nigeria, making it one of the most powerful platforms for reaching your target audience. But running ads and generating *quality* leads are two very different things. Here's what separates campaigns that convert from ones that just burn budget.

## 1. Start With the Right Objective

Most businesses make the mistake of running Traffic or Engagement campaigns when they actually want leads. Always use the **Lead Generation** or **Conversions** objective. This tells Facebook's algorithm to find people most likely to take action — not just scroll past your ad.

## 2. Nail Your Audience Targeting

Nigeria has a diverse population with very different buying behaviours across states and demographics. Don't target "Nigeria" broadly. Instead:

- Use **Custom Audiences** from your existing customer list
- Build **Lookalike Audiences** from your best customers
- Layer in interest and behaviour targeting specific to your industry

For real estate in Lagos, for example, you'd target people interested in property investment, aged 28–50, in Lagos and Abuja, with income indicators.

## 3. Your Creative Needs to Stop the Scroll

Nigerians scroll fast. Your ad has less than 2 seconds to grab attention. What works:

- **Video ads** showing real results or testimonials
- **Bold headlines** that speak directly to a pain point ("Still struggling to get clients?")
- **Social proof** — numbers, client names, before/after results

## 4. Send Traffic to a Dedicated Landing Page

Never send ad traffic to your homepage. Build a focused landing page with one clear offer and one CTA. Remove all distractions. A good landing page can double your conversion rate overnight.

## 5. Follow Up Fast

The biggest mistake Nigerian businesses make: they get a lead and wait days to follow up. Research shows that leads contacted within 5 minutes are 9x more likely to convert. Set up an automated WhatsApp or email response the moment someone submits their details.
