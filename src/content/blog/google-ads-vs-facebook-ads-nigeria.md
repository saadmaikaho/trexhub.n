One of the most common questions we get from Nigerian business owners: "Should I run Google Ads or Facebook Ads?" The honest answer is — it depends. Here's a breakdown to help you decide.

## How They're Different

**Google Ads** targets people who are *actively searching* for what you offer. Someone types "real estate agent in Lagos" into Google — your ad appears. This is called **demand capture**: you're catching people at the moment of intent.

**Facebook Ads** targets people based on *who they are* — their demographics, interests, and behaviours. Someone hasn't searched for your product, but they match your ideal customer profile. This is **demand generation**: you're creating awareness and interest.

## When to Use Google Ads

Google Ads works best when:

- People are actively searching for your product or service
- You're in a high-intent category (legal services, medical, real estate, logistics)
- You want immediate, measurable results
- Your average transaction value is high enough to justify the cost-per-click

**Best for**: Real estate, legal services, medical clinics, logistics companies, B2B services

## When to Use Facebook Ads

Facebook Ads works best when:

- Your product needs to be seen to create desire (fashion, food, lifestyle)
- You're targeting a specific demographic or interest group
- You want to build brand awareness at scale
- Your product has a broad appeal

**Best for**: E-commerce, fashion, food & beverage, events, consumer products, coaching

## The Real Answer: Use Both

The most successful businesses we work with use both platforms together:

1. **Facebook Ads** to build awareness and generate leads at the top of the funnel
2. **Google Ads** to capture people who are actively searching and ready to buy
3. **Retargeting** on both platforms to re-engage people who visited your site but didn't convert

## What About Budget?

If you're starting with a limited budget, here's our recommendation:

- **Service businesses** (real estate, consulting, B2B): Start with Google Ads
- **Product businesses** (e-commerce, fashion, FMCG): Start with Facebook Ads
- **Both**: Once you have ₦300,000+/month to invest in ads, run both
